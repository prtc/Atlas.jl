  Atmospheric parameters:

   FROM the Stromgren photometery and ATLAS9 color grids:
   Component A: Teff=12652K, log g=3.98
   The effect of the B component was not considered

   FROM observed and computed composite BALMER PROFILES:
   ATLAS9 parameters for a radii ratio (R(B)/R(A))^2=0.4
   Component A : Teff=12650K, logg=3.8
   Component B : Teff=9750K, logg=3.9 

  The radii ratio was derived from the relation:

  [R(B)/R(A)]^2=M(B)/M(A)*(g(A)/g(B))

  where M(A)/M(B)=1.7 (Gonzalez et al. 2014) and logg(A)-logg(B) was assumed equal to -0.1

  From the comparison of observed and computed spectra:
  For the A component: vsini=17 km/sec
  For the B component: vsini=6  km/sec

  Final  parameters:
  For the A component, ATLAS12 parameters: Teff=12400K, logg=3.8.
  They give a T-tauross stratification similar to that from the
  [12650, 3.8] ATLAS9 model. The ATLAS12 abundances are those 
  derived from the [12650,3.8] ATLAS9 model.
  For the B component, ATLAS9 parameters: Teff=9750K, logg=3.9 

  The parameters reproduce the composite hydrogen profiles Hbeta, Hgamma, 
  Hdelta and meet the requirement of FeI-FeII ionization equilibrium.
  Only profiles were used, no equivalent widths were measured.
