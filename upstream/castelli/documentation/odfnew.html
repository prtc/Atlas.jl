New Opacity Distribution Functions 


9 feb 2012: A few ODF were recomputed with a new release made by Bob Kurucz of the H2O line list 
            from Partridge & Schwenke.It is available at:
            http://kurucz.harvard.edu/molecules/H2O/h2ofastfix.bin  
            Recomputation of ODFs for the other metallicities is work in progress.

The ODF's are computed with the dfsynthe procedure


To convert big ODF's from ascii to binary use bigodfasctobin.com and bigodfasctobin.for
To convert little ODF's from ascii to binary use litodfasctobin.com and litodfasctobin.for

bigodfasctobin.for  bigodfasctobin.com

litodfasctobin.for  litodfasctobin.com


To convert big ODF's from  binary to ascii use bigodfbintoasc.com and bigodfbintoasc.for
To convert little ODF's from binary to ascii use litodfbintoasc.com and litodfbintoasc.for

bigodfbintoasc.for  bigodfbintoasc.com

litodfbintoasc.for  litodfbintoasc.com


To interpolate big ODFs for metallicity or microturbulence use dfinterpbig.com and dfinterpbig.for
To interpolate little ODFs for metallicity or microturbulence use dfinterplit.com and dfinterplit.for
BINARY ODFs must be used !!!!!!!!!!!!

The interpolation formula for x0< x_int < x1  is:

f(x_int)=[(x1-x_int)/(x1-x0)]f(x0)+ [(x_int-x0)/(x1-x0)]f(x1)
f(x_int)=          w1        f(x0)+           w2        f(x1)


dfinterpbig.for  dfinterpbig.com

dfinterplit.for  dfinterplit.com


w1 and w2 are input data in dfinterpbig.com and dfinterplit.com
